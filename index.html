Television

  • Became available in crude experimental forms in the late 1920s
  • In 2013, 79% of the world's households owned a television set
  • Etymology: Ancient Greek tèle (far) and Latin visio (sight)

Clara Luper

  • Best known for her leadership role in the 1958 Oklahoma City sit-in movement
  • Taught American history for 41 years

Ivy

  • Genus: Hedera
  • Cultivation:
    • Aesthetic evergreen foliage
    • Attracting wildlife
    • Adaptable design uses in narrow planting spaces
  • Several ivy species have become a serious invasive species

Cantilevers

  • Rigid structural element anchored at one end to a support from which it protrudes
  • Widely found in construction
    • bridges
    • balconies

Giraffes

  • Tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant
  • Neck can be up to 2–2.4 m (6.6–7.9 ft) in length
  • Neck vertebrae have ball and socket joints
  • Eat about 34 kg (75 lb) of foliage daily
